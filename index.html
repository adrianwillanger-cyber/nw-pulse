Why I built it

Off-the-shelf tools ship confident answers that are quietly wrong.

I used Altos. I used the popular brokerage portals. Every one of them was good enough to mislead at the neighborhood scale: comps drawn by ZIP across a school-boundary line, condo estimates that forgot HOA dues, descriptions that sounded like steering. At my scale — Wedgwood vs. Maple Leaf, Pinehurst vs. Lake City — they weren't precise enough to publish.

So the pipeline and the judgment ship together.

NW Pulse is the engine I built to feed every dashboard, every public brief, and every social export I send. It's NWMLS-clean by default — aggregate-only on anything that leaves my workspace. And it's wired so the broker reading the numbers is the same person who runs the ingest. The judgment ships with the data.

API surface

A small, opinionated set of endpoints.

Every route is authenticated except health. Responses are JSON, paginated where appropriate, and shaped for the consumers I actually run — not a generic data platform.

Compliance posture

NWMLS-clean by default.

Anything that leaves the workspace is aggregate-only. Per-listing detail stays behind the X-Api-Key wall. The conservative posture is the default — not something I have to remember.

What ships publicly

  • Counts & medians per ZIP
  • Trend lines on aggregate metrics
  • Pre-computed Broker's Read commentary
  • Cross-zip comparisons at the ZIP level

What stays gated

  • Per-listing addresses & MLS numbers
  • Seller/agent contact information
  • Raw NWMLS field exports
  • Anything that requires a paid NWMLS license downstream
